Preparing for the Final Assessment

Assignment: Final Assessment – Part One

Due Date: Last day of week 14, 11:55 p.m. See Calendar or Instructional Plan.
Value of Assignment: 5% of final grade
Passing Grade: 55%
Length: 250-300 words
Submission Instructions: Submit your work as a .doc/.docx or .pdf file to the assignment submission folder titled “Final Assessment – Part One”. Please do not submit your work as a zipped folder.
Evaluation: See below.

This first part of the final assignment is an opportunity for you to read, analyze, and summarize an article. You will find the article in the next topic.
This is an individual assignment.

Rationale

This assignment is an opportunity to demonstrate your learning by:

  • Identifying purpose, audience, and thesis in a variety of texts
  • Summarizing college-level readings to identify main idea and key points
  • Applying knowledge of standard Canadian English Grammar, spelling, and punctuation to academic and professional writing

Evaluation

The rubric for assessing this assignment is here (quicklink to pdf or Word document).

The policy for late submission of student work can be found in the Course Information module in this course.

Directions

    1Read the assigned article in the next topic.

    2Answer the following questions (steps 3 to 5) on “Work Sheet for Final Assignment or on an original document. If you are not using the provided document, label your responses clearly.

    3Identify the intended audience for the assigned article. Explain your reasoning. (4 points)

    • This question is worth 20% of the Part One grade. It’s therefore reasonable to conclude that it is possible to successfully respond to these questions in 3-4 sentences.
    • If you look at the rubric for this question, you will see that complete marks can only be awarded to responses that “correctly identify and explain” the text’s audience. You can access the rubric below in Evaluation or in the Submission Folder for this assignment.

    4Paraphrase the thesis of the assigned article and explain how you identified it. (4 points)

    • The same points with respect to writing and grading identified in step 3 apply to this step as well.
    • Notice that you are asked to paraphrase the thesis, which means present the author’s thesis in your own words.

    5Write a concise summary of the article (150-250 words), focusing only on the author’s main point and key supporting points. Include only ideas or information that is in the original text, and remember to use your own words. (12 points)

    • This question is worth 12 points out of a total of 20. That means it is worth 60% of the Part One grade. You can therefore conclude that we are expecting a longer response here. As it is a summary, it should be approximately 1/3-1/4 the length of the original text.
    • If you look at the rubric for this question, you will see that equal weight is assigned to all the criteria. You can access the rubric below in Evaluation or in the Submission Folder for this assignment.

    6Be sure to read over your work carefully before you send it off to your professor, so you can check that your ideas read as clearly on the page as they sounded in your head.
