Persuasive Writing — Part 2

Analysis of Persuasive Text

Now that you’ve seen how a piece of persuasive writing can be analyzed according to its key features, try to do it on your own. Read the following text and follow the prompts to identify the features and techniques of argumentation that we’ve been learning about in this module.

Answer the following questions:

  • Which of three persuasive strategies — credibility (ethos), logic (logos), or emotion (pathos) — does the author seem to emphasize?
  • How does the author establish his or her credibility, i.e., knowledge, reliability, or fairness?
  • What is the author’s perspective toward the topic? Does this bias affect the author’s credibility?
  • Which kinds of logical evidence draw on support his or her argument?
  • What emotional techniques does the author use, i.e., narration, description, or evocative language?
