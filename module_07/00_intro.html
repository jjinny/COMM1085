Persuasive Writing — Part 2

Week 07 Introduction

In week 6, we focused on identifying what makes an effective persuasive argument. This week we will look more closely at the skills and concepts employed by effective persuasive writers.

Our work will focus on the body of your argument and the kinds of support you can use to persuade your reader. Support, or ‘proof’, can be organized into three different kinds or categories, depending on the effect that it is meant to have on the reader:

  • Ethos: Supporting material that helps the reader trust the author
  • Logos: Supporting material that persuades the reader based on logic
  • Pathos: Supporting material that persuades the reader based on emotion

This theory was developed over 2000 years ago by the Greek philosopher Aristotle, so the categories use the same terms as they originally were in Greek.

At the end of this discussion, you’ll have an opportunity to employ your persuasive writing skills by writing a persuasive message of your own.
