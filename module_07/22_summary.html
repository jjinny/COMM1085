Persuasive Writing — Part 2

Week 07 Summary

Writing persuasively involves making the most of three different types of persuasive strategies:

Ethos: This strategy establishes the credibility of the writer by demonstrating

  • Knowledge
  • Reliability
  • Fairness

A writer can increase their credibility by using citations and references to demonstrate the credibility of their research sources. Also, acknowledging and limiting bias are both important.

Logos: This strategy appeals to the reader’s sense of logic by using correct

  • Induction
  • Deduction
  • Causal and effect relationships
  • Analogies
  • Definitions
  • Statistics

A writer can improve the logic of their argument by avoiding common logical fallacies such as ad hominem attacks, slippery slope arguments, black and white arguments, etc.

Pathos: This strategy engages the emotions of the reader by using

  • Narration
  • Description
  • Evocative language
  • Sentence variety
  • Diction
