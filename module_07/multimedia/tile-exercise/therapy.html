Many of the basic principles of good quality therapy styles are found within the area of sex therapy. It is unique only in that its focus is treating sexual problems and concerns. Individual sex therapists will differ considerably in their basic treatment approaches. What is common is that the need for sex therapy is brief and highly effective.

The Nature of the Problem Sex therapy addresses both the physical problem and the emotional issues that underlie it. Treatments depend on the nature of the sexual dysfunction. For example: Treating erectile dysfunction differs tremendously from someone questioning their sexual orientation.
Realistic Therapeutic Goals It is imperative that the therapy is grounded realistically. Reestablishing emotional intimacy within a relationship is a reasonable goal. A 75 year old man wanting erections like he had in his youth is an unrealistic goal.
Success!
Therapist's Skills Success in therapy is tied to the therapeutic alliance between the client and his/her therapist. A strong alliance includes: therapist genuineness, unconditional positive regard, empathy, shared goals, and (some studies suggest) the ability to integrate humour.
Client's Motivation A lack of desire to participate, cooperate or to be honest and authentic will limit the success of sex therapy. This is why there is such a low success rate in mandated therapies.
