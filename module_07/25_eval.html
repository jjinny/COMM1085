Persuasive Writing — Part 2

Assignment: Persuasive Message

Due Date: Last day of week 7, 11:55 p.m. See Calendar or Instructional Plan.
Value of Assignment: 10% of final grade
Passing Grade: 55%
Length: 500-600 words (minimum)
Submission Instructions: Submit your work as a .doc/.docx or .pdf file to the assignment submission folder titled “Persuasive Message Assignment”. Please do not submit your work as a zipped folder.
Evaluation: See below.

This assignment is an opportunity for you to compose an argument that convinces your audience to accept the legitimacy of your position or persuades them to accept your position as the correct one.

This is an individual assignment.

Rationale

This assignment is an opportunity to write a persuasive message and apply APA@Conestoga style by:

  • Expressing a point of view in response to a written prompt
  • Applying the three-step process (plan, write, revise) to create a variety of academic and workplace documents
  • Applying knowledge of standard Canadian English Grammar, spelling, and punctuation to academic and professional writing
  • Integrating sources using appropriate documentation format

Directions

    11. Compose a persuasive message (essay, letter, memo) that convinces your audience to adopt your point of view or take action on an issue.

    2Integrate two external sources that support your position.

    3Establish a clear purpose before you begin writing your message.

    4Carefully consider the interests, values, and needs of your audience, which could be:

    • Peers – classmates, co-workers
    • Leaders – parents, professors, employers, elected officials
    • Dependents — a client / customer or a potential client / customer
    • A demographic – might be defined by geography (e.g. a country), age (e.g. belonging to a particular generation), or behaviour (e.g. consumers, voters)

    5Use the persuasive techniques and strategies covered in eConestoga to write the first draft of your persuasive message (500-600 word, minimum). Anticipate and respond to potential resistance from your reader.

    6Use APA@Conestoga format for your analysis:

    • Use 12-point Arial font.
    • Double space your text.
    • Include a title page according to this format from The Learning Commons.
    • Cite your sources following APA@Conestoga documentation style, including in-text citations and a references page.

    7Revise your draft based to ensure it reads clearly and that it follows the rubric, the assignment instructions, and the resources provided in eConestoga in weeks 6 and 7.
