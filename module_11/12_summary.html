Organizing Research — Part 1

Week 11 Summary

1 This week, we looked at the next stage of the research paper: the gathering and organizing of information. The best tool for this stage is the outline. Creating an outline allows us to

  • find information
  • evaluate ideas
  • organize ideas

2 In the outlining stage, we turn the research question into a thesis statement, or controlling idea. As we organize our ideas, we create headings and sub-headings that show how the information is organized and how each section relates back to the thesis or controlling idea. In a report, these headings and sub-headings can be used in the final draft to help your reader follow your organization more clearly.

3 As you create your outline, you should decide what kind of report you are writing, as this will affect how you organize your information.

  • Informational -- provides research information in way that makes it more accessible for the reader
  • Analytic -- provides research information and then analyzes its significance for the reader
  • Persuasive -- provides information and analysis for the purpose of persuading the reader to think or act in a specific way

4 This week also included a review of punctuation: commas, colons, semi-colons, and apostrophes.
