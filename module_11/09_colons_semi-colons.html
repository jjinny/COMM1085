Organizing Research — Part 1

Colons and Semi-colons

“The colon (:) is another punctuation mark used to indicate a full stop. You can use a colon to introduce lists, quotes, explanations, and examples.” (Writing for Success, Chapter 3.3, “Colons”)

“The semi-colon (;) also indicates a break in the flow of a sentence, but functions differently than a period or comma. When you encounter a semi-colon while reading aloud, this represents a good place to pause and take a break.” (Writing for Success, Chapter 3.2, “Semi-colons”)

Colon

Use a colon at the end of a complete statement to introduce any of the following:

  • A list
    →
    I have three dogs: Mitzy, Fluffy, and Fred.


  • An explanation / example
    →
    Here’s one way to spend less: only shop once a week.


  • A quotation
    Use a colon to introduce a quotation when you want to use a complete sentence to establish context for the quotation.

    →
    The stereotype of the Canadian experience can even be seen in the words of former Prime Minister Pierre Trudeau: “Canada is a country whose main exports are hockey players and cold fronts.”

Semi-colon

“Semi-colons separate things. Most commonly, they separate two main clauses that are closely related to each other but that could stand on their own as sentences if you wanted them to.” (Mignon Fogarty aka Grammar Girl, Semi-colons, “Use semi-colons to separate things and add variety”)

  • To replace a period, a semi colon can appear between independent clauses that are closely related in meaning.
    →
    Mario was late; he missed his bus.




  • To be used before a transitional expression (such as however, next, therefore, as a result) that logically links two clauses
    →
    I’d like to help you; however, I’m late for class.




  • To separate three or more items in a series if any of the items already contains commas (a COMPLEX LIST).

    →
    For a successful fishing trip, you need the right equipment, which need not be expensive; knowledge of fish habitat and habits; and, of course, hungry fish.
