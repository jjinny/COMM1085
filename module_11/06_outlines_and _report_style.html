Organizing Research — Part 1

Outlines and Report Style

Using headings and sub-headings in our outline can also allow us to organize the final draft of the research paper. In a “report style,” the headings and sub-headings that we use in our outline can remain in the final draft of our paper.

While different from a formal report, which requires the writer to include several specific features, a report style paper that uses headings and sub-headings can help you to present your research clearly: it emphasizes the organization of your ideas, which helps your reader to absorb the information quickly.

Headings and sub-headings are a useful organizational tool for various purposes, audiences, and professional sectors.

Audiences

Report style is equally suitable for audiences within your organization and without.

Internal Audiences

  • From junior members to senior members
    • project updates
    • proposals
  • From senior members to the rest of the organization
    • Defining policy
    • Giving instructions
    • Delivering Professional development/ training

External Audiences

  • Current and potential investors / shareholders
  • Current and potential clients / customers
  • Government agencies or other regulatory bodies
