Organizing Research — Part 1

Activity: Organizing an Outline

Take the following paragraph and create an outline for it. The paragraph belongs in a report that attempts to answer: “What kind of impact smart technology is having on the modern home.”

Writing an outline is different from writing a draft of a paper. In fact, it might be more accurate to think of it as a sorting exercise rather than a writing exercise. The goal is to put relevant pieces of information into logical groups. We can use headings and sub-headings to help us organize our groups of information. The headings and sub-headings act like “buckets” or folders that we can use to contain the information that we acquire.

When we have headings and sub-headings organized in advance, we’re able to search for information freely. It’s no longer necessary to start with the introduction and force ourselves to work from top to bottom. Instead, we can simply place information in the appropriate location as the information becomes available.

For sections of the same importance, use the same level of heading. By putting information in the appropriate place, you can see when you have gathered enough information to sufficiently explain what you’re trying to say. It also becomes easier for you to ensure that you have presented your ideas logically. If the connections between ideas are unclear, you can rearrange them or add explanations to clarify the relationship between them.
