Organizing Research — Part 1

Week 11 Introduction

This week we will help you to transition from the proposal email to developing a preliminary outline of your research paper.

First, we’ll reinforce the relationship between your research question and your thesis statement and share with you relevant examples of student work.

Next, we’ll think about the purpose and audience of an outline document, and then we’ll conclude by looking at techniques that you can use to write and organize an outline that will ultimately lead to a more successful research paper

Learning Outcomes

Learning Outcomes

Throughout this chapter, you are building the skills to:

  • 1.2 Apply pre-reading skills and strategies
  • 2.1 Identify thesis, main ideas, and supporting details in a reading
  • 3.2 Evaluate reasons and evidence in a written document
  • 3.3 Distinguish fact from opinion
  • 4.6 Edit written work for clarity, correctness, conciseness, tone, and accuracy
