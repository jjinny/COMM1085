Google vs Online Databases

Google’s search engine creates an index of the content on the Internet and uses algorithms to deliver results to users. Google is immensely popular worldwide: in 2015, Google accounted for 75% share of the global search market.

But an online search engine may not necessarily be the best tool for gathering the information you need to learn and write at the post-secondary level. By contrast, Conestoga’s collection of online databases is designed explicitly for this purpose.

Watch these videos to learn the difference between a library database and an internet search engine.
