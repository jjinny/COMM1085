Value of Reflection

Take the following short quiz to discover the value of reflection and self-assessment.

Reflection is an integral component of the learning process. The interpretations we make about our own learning experience can help us to change habits, correct errors, and solve problems that will make our next learning experience even more successful (Mezirow, 1990, p. 1).

Put another way, reflection represents “the thread that makes continuity of learning possible” (Rodgers, 2002, p. 845), and for this reason, reflection and self-assessment have become increasingly common components of best practice guidelines in the workplace. The following fields are likely to expect that their members engage in the process of reflecting on, asking questions about, evaluating their own performance:

  • nursing
  • social work
  • early childhood education
  • policing

Now, you try:

Can you think of a situation in your own field where you might be expected to reflect on something you’ve made or something you’ve done in order to identify areas for growth? Have you had to do this for other courses in your secondary or post-secondary education, or in a co-op placement? What did you find helpful…difficult…surprising…about the experience?
