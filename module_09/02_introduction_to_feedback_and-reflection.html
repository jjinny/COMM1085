The learning habits and activities represented in these scenarios all work toward the same objective: building “metacognition.” Metacognition refers to our ability to think about our own thoughts. On the surface, this ability to reflect on our own learning might not seem significant. But new research into the way that humans learn suggests otherwise: that reflecting critically on our previous learning experiences has a powerful impact on our ability to improve our future actions.

Our work this week will help you to better understand this connection between past learning and future action and introduce you to a variety of self-reflection models that you can use to make your own self-reflection more productive.
