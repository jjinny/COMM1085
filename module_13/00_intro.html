Completing the Research Process — Part 1

Week 13 Introduction

Our work this week will focus on the final stages of the Research Project process. As you develop the contents of your outline into a complete and polished draft, these activities will help you to review and refine your writing.

In particular, we’ll work to better understand the differences between similar-sounding terms like revision, editing, and proofreading.

We’ll identify specific areas of your writing to review — such as APA@Conestoga guidelines for in-text citations — and suggest various techniques that you can use to make your review process more effective.

After several weeks of planning, research, and organizing your ideas, you’re approaching the final stage of the writing process: you’ve brought all of your ideas together, in complete sentences and well-developed paragraphs, to produce a first draft.

However, it’s important to realize that this first draft can still be improved upon. Before they present their writing to their audiences, effective writers read what they’ve written, identify changes that need to be made, and then make the changes.
