Completing the Research Process — Part 1

Understanding the Review Process

In order to be successful at the review stage, it’s helpful to understand what’s going in our brains when we read our own writing. Wired’s Why It’s So Hard to Catch Your Own Typos explains why it often feels easier to spot mistakes in other people’s writing than in our own. Reviewing our writing is a skill that can be learned and practiced, rather than a talent that someone is simply “good” or “bad” at it.

You may have heard this review stage described in a number of different ways, such as revision, editing, or proofreading. In fact, each of these terms has a specific meaning. As you’ll see below, each one focusses on a particular aspect of your writing, and, combined, they represent a complete review process (Engineering Communication Program, 2017).
