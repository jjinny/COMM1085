Completing the Research Process — Part 1

Revising Your Assignment

When you review your research project during revision, that is, when you look at your text as a whole, take the following steps:

    1Confirm that you’ve met the requirements of the assignment.

    • Have you met the length requirements of the assignment?
    • Does your writing meet the appropriate purpose for this assignment, e.g., to inform, to persuade, etc.?
    • Have you explored the topic and research question that your instructor approved in response to your proposal email or in subsequent conversations?

    2Check support for major claims.

    • Have you consulted experts to confirm that claims you’ve made are factual?
    • Have you provided specific examples to illustrate how your claims might look?
    • Have you avoided making vague or unprovable claims?

    3Check your text against your outline.

    • Have you included all of the supporting information that you originally gathered?
    • If not, has that omission left a gap that you need to address?
    • Did you address any notes that your instructor made in regard to your outline?

    4Evaluate the organization and development of your text.

    • Do the paragraphs of your paper fit logically with one another?
    • Is it clear to the reader why each paragraph follows the one that came before?
    • Has your focus on answering the original question remained consistent throughout the paper?

    5Check unity and coherence on a paragraph level.

    • Have you used topic and concluding sentences to give your paragraphs structure?
    • Do you support your topic sentence logically, with details that focus only on the development of one main idea?
    • Have you used transitional words and phrases to build continuity between sentences?
