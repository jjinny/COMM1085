During a busy semester, with multiple school assignments and personal responsibilities competing for time and energy, many students find it difficult to budget time for this kind of review process. Yet, the idea of review — checking to make sure something is correct — is one that’s common in other aspects of our lives.

The following examples represent situations in our personal or working lives that might be considered similar to the process of checking our writing. For each situation, describe how often you might conduct this “check”.

Each of these scenarios describe situations where we want to check what we’re doing:

  • We check our appearance because we want to make a good first impression
  • We check the email address because we don’t to insult anybody or embarrass ourselves
  • We check our luggage because we don’t want to be stranded without socks or prevented from boarding our plane.

While these scenarios drawn from everyday life might illustrate a kind of “checking” that many people can relate to, our main focus this week is the review of written work in academic and professional situations. How might your work in these situations require or expect you to review your writing before it’s complete?
