Activity: Critical Thinking in the Workplace

We’ve heard from employers and employment sites that communication and critical thinking skills are in demand. What does it actually look like to use these skills on the job? In what situations of scenarios might you find yourself drawing on the learning outcomes that we’re focused on this course?

Match the following workplace scenario to the seven COMM1085 learning outcomes.

Answer Options:

A Use reading skills to understand the audience, purpose, and main idea of various texts.
B Use critical thinking skills to respond to questions of bias, evidence and source in a variety of texts.
C Summarize video, texts, ideas or designs.
D Give my point of view in response to a problem or question.
E Plan, write, and revise written pieces of work.
F Use standard Canadian English grammar in all of my communications.
G Locate and use appropriate sources to support my opinions and reference these sources in an appropriate manner.
