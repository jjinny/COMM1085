Introduction to College Reading and Writing

Welcome to College Reading and Writing Skills

We’ll get started this week by exploring the following questions:

  • How might reading, writing, and critical thinking skills be useful in the workplace?
  • What strategies help us to effectively read informational texts, like textbooks, newspapers articles, and reports?
  • What questions can we ask about an informational text to help us better understand and evaluate it?

But first, let’s take a moment to review a few terms that you may have encountered in your previous communications studies. Click on each picture below to see a description of each term.

Text

Any collection of ideas and information that’s been produced to communicate with an audience. In this course, we’ll look at a variety of texts, including those that rely heavily on the printed word, such as newspaper articles, as well as those that are more visual, such as videos.

Critical Thinking

The process of making judgments about ideas using reasoning rather than emotion. Various techniques might include considering alternative perspectives, comparing similar ideas, gathering reliable information, making observations, and using specific criteria to evaluate ideas.

Source

Documentation

The process of acknowledging your sources, using a particular system or style that defines what information needs to be acknowledged and how that information will be presented to the reader. Styles that you may have heard of previously include APA, MLA, and IEEE.
