Summary Writing — Part 1

VideoPlus: Using Text References

When we write for an academic or professional purpose, such as informing or persuading, it’s likely that our audience will expect our writing to contain reliable information. To meet this expectation, we often turn to experts and reporters and other reliable sources of information. When we include information from these kinds of sources in our own writing, we are supporting our own ideas with "text references".

There are two ways to include or "integrate" text references in your own writing: quoting and paraphrasing.

Quoting

Using the exact words from another source in your own writing

Paraphrasing

Using your own words to communicate an idea from another source
