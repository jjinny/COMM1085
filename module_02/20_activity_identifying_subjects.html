Summary Writing — Part 1

Activity: Identifying Subjects

As we continue exploring the building blocks of grammar, we’ll now look specifically at two necessary components of a complete sentence: a subject and a verb. To determine if a sentence is complete, you can first look for the subject, which we can define generally as "what the sentence is about".

Only nouns and pronouns can be the subject of a sentence, so we will need to use our knowledge of parts of speech to identify the subject of a sentence.

Nouns

Nouns name:

  • A person (Monica) or people (students)
  • A place (restaurant) or places (countries)
  • A thing (computer) or things (desks)
  • An idea (intelligence) or ideas (solutions)

Pronouns

Pronouns replace nouns. Examples include:

  • Personal pronouns (I, you, he, she, it, we, and they)
  • Indefinite pronouns (everyone, no one, somebody, anything)
