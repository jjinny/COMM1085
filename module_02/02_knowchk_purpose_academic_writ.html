Summary Writing — Part 1

Knowledge Check: The Purpose of Academic Writing

To help us better understand the purpose of academic writing, think back on our discussion of workplace writing from last week.

Just as you need to

Persuade… a client to renew their subscription to your company’s software

Document… the results of required pre-op inspections prior to operating heavy equipment

Record… the details of a customer complaint for a supervisor’s review

Recommend… that your client should choose interlocking paving stone rather than stamped concrete for their home renovation.

in the workplace, you need to do the same things in your courses at Conestoga.

In other words, both academic and professional writing are motivated by very practical and legitimate purposes. The following are some common purposes for academic writing:

  • To demonstrate your understanding of new concepts
  • To apply new skills
  • To participate in a conversation of ideas
  • To practice specific writing techniques or styles that you’ll be expected to use on the job

For example, the purpose of a summary, which you will complete in Week 3, is to capture the main ideas and key supporting points of a text. In this kind of writing, just as in the workplace examples above, you must demonstrate your understanding of required information and record it concisely and accurately.
