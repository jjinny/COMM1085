Summary Writing — Part 1

Paraphrasing

The video in Using Text References described two fundamental tools of academic writing: quoting and paraphrasing. These tools help us use other people’s words and ideas to support and develop our own writing.

Now we will focus on recognizing an effective paraphrase. Paraphrasing is a very effective way to make your own writing more informative or more interesting by including other people’s ideas or research without using quotations. You can incorporate the ideas in your own words so that they fit seamlessly into your writing.

Effective paraphrasing is also an essential aspect of summary writing. Next week, you will continue to develop your paraphrasing skills as you work to summarize an assigned text.

Paraphrasing uses the original author’s ideas instead of the exact words. In order to paraphrase effectively, we need to understand a few additional criteria. An effective paraphrase should also:

  • be about the same length as the original source,
  • never include any new ideas or facts that are not in the original, and
  • avoid using synonyms that alter the tone or meaning of the original.

Let’s look at an effective and an ineffective example of the same original sentence.

Original Sentence:

The extent to which automation will affect overall employment remains controversial.

Sorenson, 2013, para. 8

Example 1:

The amplitude to which computerization will impinge overal lenrollment remains litigious (Sorenson, 2013, para. 8.)

Is this paraphrase effective? Click the arrow to check your answer.

Analysis 1

This paraphrase is ineffective.

This writer has used a thesaurus to replace key words with other similar words in order to avoid repeating three of the original words in a row.

  • "Extent" became "amplitude"
  • "Automation" became "computerization"
  • "Affect" became "impinge"
  • "Employment" became "enrollment"
  • "Controversial" became "litigious"

The result is ineffective because many of the new words carry a different meaning from the original. Combined, the words produce a sentence that’s even more dense and difficult to understand than the original sentence.

Example 2

Experts are still debating whether employment will be hurt by automation in the long run (Sorenson, 2013, para.8)

Is this paraphrase effective? Click the arrow to check your answer.

Analysis 2

This paraphrase is effective.

Although this paraphrase includes a few individual words from the original, such as "employment" and "automation", it avoids using phrases like "the extent to which".

It also presents the ideas in a different order than the original. The original sentence ended with "controversial", while the paraphrase begins with "experts are still debating".

The meaning and the intent of the message are retained while the words and order have changed.

Example:

Analysis

This paraphrase is ineffective. It is too brief and needs to capture more of the original’s detail.

Example 2
Example:

Ontario Labour Minister Kevin Flynn suggested that union membership is good for workers, but he fails to mention the high cost of membership.

Analysis

This paraphrase is ineffective. It includes new ideas that are not in the original.

Example 3
Example:

Ontario Labour Minister Kevin Flynn suggested union membership benefits workers by producing higher wages and more job security, two things that have been lost in today’s global economy. He also says that new kinds of business organizations have made it more challenging for workers to come together and form unions, especially because the old-fashioned rules don’t reflect today’s business world.

Analysis

This paraphrase is ineffective. It repeats too closely the same sentence structure and organization patterns as the original.

Example 4
Example:

The rise of franchise businesses and fragmented workplaces has made it more difficult for workers to form unions, according to Ontario Labour Minster Kevin Flynn. He expressed concern about today’s changing workplace and world, suggesting higher living standards and employment protections will be eroded if union membership continues to decline.

Analysis

This paraphrase is ineffective. It changes the original sentence structure but retains many of the same phrases.

Example 5
Example:

Ontario Labour Minister Kevin Flynn is calling for updated policy on unionization. He suggests that existing labour policy needs to be updated to reflect the kinds of businesses and workplaces that we see today, and so more workers can enjoy a high quality of life and job security.

Analysis

This paraphrase is effective.
