Reflection: Documentation and Citation

In academic and professional writing, it is sometimes necessary to support our own ideas and interpretations with information and opinions developed by other people. Documentation is the practice of acknowledging those outside sources of information and providing our reader with information about them. There are many different systems or styles of documentation, which guide us to acknowledge our sources in a particular way. When we provide our reader with the required information about a source, we are creating a citation.

To ensure we correctly document and cite the outside sources that we include in our academic writing, we need to understand the following:

  • How to incorporate ideas and words from other people
  • How to tell your reader that you’ve incorporated ideas from other people
  • How to show your reader where these borrowed ideas came from and where they can be found

You might think of documentation and citation guidelines as a system that’s used in communication to handle information safely. After all, every discipline or field of work has its own rules and guidelines, and these rules and guidelines are often designed and enforced for the safety of various groups of people: workers, customers, citizens, children, etc.

Think about your own field: are there tools that would be dangerous if you didn’t use them correctly? Materials that could cause harm if not handled safely? By-laws, codes, statutes or federal legislation that you’re obligated to know and follow in order to do your job or run your business safely and legally?

What can you learn about the writer’s life based on what is included and what is left out? What can you tell about the writer’s personality from the tone, choice of words, and structure of the sentence?

In the same way, academic writing is governed by guidelines and standards to ensure that readers understand exactly who produced the ideas they are presented with in a text. So, it’s important to understand that students are expected to follow documentation and citation guidelines in the same that they employees in a particular field of work are expected to follow certain regulations, codes, and standards.
