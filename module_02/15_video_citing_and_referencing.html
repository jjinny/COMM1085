Summary Writing — Part 1

VideoPlus: Citing and Referencing in APA@Conestoga

All style guides include guidelines that instruct writers how to manage the information they use from other sources. Whenever you use another person’s ideas or exact words, you need to give your reader two pieces of information:

  • A sign that the words or ideas they’re reading came from someone else.
  • Information about the source of those words or ideas so they can find it if they want to.

In APA@Conestoga style, this information is provided to the reader in two places. The two work together, so both must be included.

In-text citations

An in-text citation includes only three particular pieces of information about the source of information (author, year, and location in text) that you have used. It appears in your writing, immediately after the idea or words that you use.

A references page

The references page appears on a separate page, at the end of your writing. It lists all of the sources that you’ve used in your writing, and it provides more information about each source than what appears in the in-text citation.
