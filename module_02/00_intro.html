Summary Writing — Part 1

Week 2 Introduction

Welcome back!

Last week we considered the usefulness of reading and writing skills both in school and in the workplace. This week we’ll look more closely at academic writing:

  • How is it distinct from other kinds of writing?
  • What is our purpose and who is our audience when we’re writing in this style?
  • How do we incorporate other people’s ideas and information in our writing?
  • What does “academic integrity” mean in the context of academic writing?

After we explore these questions, we’ll conclude this week’s work by reviewing the fundamental building blocks of the English language: parts of speech. This review will help to prepare you for our first graded assessment in the course: Language Lab #1.

Learning Outcomes

Learning Outcomes

After you complete this week's learning, you should know how to:

  • 1.2 Apply pre-reading skills and strategies
  • 1.3 Identify purpose and audience for various readings
  • 2.1 Identify thesis, main ideas, and supporting details in reading
  • 4.6 Edit written work for clarity, correctness, conciseness, tone, and accuracy
  • 6.5 Apply an appropriate style of documentation to avoid plagiarism
