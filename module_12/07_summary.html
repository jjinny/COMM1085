Completing the Research Process — Part 1

Week 13 Summary

This week, we focused on the revision process by breaking it down into three important stages.

1 Revising: This stage looks at the organization of the paper as a whole.

  • Is there enough support for each point?
  • Does every main point relate back to the controlling idea or thesis introduced in the introduction?
  • Would the points of support be more effective if presented in a different order?

2 Editing: This stage looks at the individual sentences.

  • Is every sentence clear?
  • Is there enough sentence variety?
  • Are the important ideas emphasized by the position and wording of the sentences they are in?

3 Proofreading: This final stage is a last check for grammar, punctuation, and spelling errors. Don't rely on your software program to catch all your errors. Try the following:

  • Leave time between your editing stage and proofreading stage to let your brain forget what it thinks it knows is on the page.
  • Read your paper out loud.
  • Read from the bottom to the top, one sentence at a time.
  • Check for commonly confused words that spellcheck would not catch.
