Organizing Research — Part 2

Activity:Integrating Text References

Notice that the assignment this week is not simply called “an outline”. Instead, we’re asking you for a “comprehensive” outline, which means that your outline must include nearly all of the elements that your final paper will include.

Although you are not expected to write in full sentences and paragraphs for this assignment, you are expected to include all of the text references, i.e., quoted and paraphrased information, that your paper needs to be clear, informative, and compelling.

Remember from our discussion of “source types” in Week 8 that different kinds of sources have different kinds of information:

  • Reference materials → factual, historical context
  • Newspapers → detailed accounts of recent events, informed interpretation and discussion of current issues
  • Trade journals → technical details, professional insight

In this way, not all text references are equally effective within the context of your research. Think about the appropriateness of each source and whether it will help your reader to understand or agree with you (depending on your purpose).

Activity: Knowledge Check on Best Resources

Imagine that you are writing a paper that attempts to answer the question: “What should people consider before getting a tattoo?” You have identified three points that require support from text references:

  • Pick a design that has special meaning in your life. But be careful: One could choose an image or quote that they end up regretting.
  • It’s crucial that you ensure the tattoo shop you visit is licensed and sanitary. You don’t want to run the risk of getting an infection, which is more likely if the facility isn’t properly run.
  • After getting tattooed, the main thing you need to keep in mind is taking care of it during the healing process.

Match the type of information or evidence that would best support each point and the best source to find that evidence.
