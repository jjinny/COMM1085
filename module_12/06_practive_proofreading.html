Completing the Research Process — Part 1

Best Practices for Proofreading

The final step for reviewing your text is proofreading. At this point you are looking for errors of grammar, punctuation, spelling, etc.

  • Use spell and grammar check on your word processor…but be aware of its limitations:
    • Unless you have changed your program’s default settings, it will likely identify Canadian spelling as incorrect (e.g., colour).
    • It won’t be able to alert you if you have misspelled a person’s name
    • It may not catch typos if the mistaken word is still technically a word
  • Check for commonly confused words
    • English spelling can be idiosyncratic due to the history of particular words and the complexity of its overall development
    • Review these helpful resources on Commonly Confused Words and Spelling in Writing for Success

General Tips for Proofreading

  • Leave time between first draft and review
  • Work from a printed page to avoid the limitations of spelling and grammar checks. If you can’t print, change the appearance of the screen (font, background colour)
  • Read your draft out loud.
  • Read your draft from the bottom to the top, one paragraph at a time.
