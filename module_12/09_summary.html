Organizing Research — Part 2

Week 12 Summary

This week we took an in-depth look at comprehensive outlines, which include specific support in the form of references to your research sources.

An effective comprehensive outline has the following components:

1 Headings that clearly identify the different parts of the paper

2 Point form lists of supports for each body paragraph. Point form writing is

  • condensed, perhaps even using abbreviations and symbols
  • parallel
  • bulleted or numbered

3 References to research sources that have been chosen to provide the right type of evidence to support each point

4 An introduction that provides a thesis statement as well as

  • any necessary definitions
  • some background information
  • reasons why the reader should care about the topic

5 A conclusion that summarizes key points but also

  • discusses the implications of this information
  • suggests how the information can be used
  • identifies questions that need more research
