Using Point-Form Style

Point-form style varies from the style that you will use for writing your final paper. Which characteristics of point-form style can you spot in the following sample?

A. Reasons Krispy Kreme’s First expansion into Canada Failed

Reason #1: High Costs

Topic Sentence: Krispy Kreme’s First attempt to expand into Canada failed due to unexpectedly high costs
Explanation, proof, or example:

  • Stores were too expensive to operate → high upkeep and real estate costs
  • Stores were too big → around 5000 square feet each (Harris, 2016)
  • Stores were too expensive → cost up to 2-3 million dollars to open (Shaw, 2016).

Reason #2: Poor Sales

Topic Sentence: Krispy Kreme’s first attempt to expand into Canada failed due to sales peaking early, resulting in overproduction amid rapid expansion.
Explanation, proof, or example:

  • Sales of donuts were high at beginning → not enough to justify costs over time (Wright, 2017).
  • Sales declined after its first few years in Canada.

Although there are common features to point-form style, the depth of detail in an outline can vary from basic to advanced. The more detail you include in your outline, the easier it will be to write a full and insightful paper. Let’s compare two outlines on the same topic and the corresponding paragraph.

Basic Depth
Paragraph Outline

Topic sentence: Living close to work has many advantages

  1. Financial benefits
    • Spend less on gas
    • Spend less on car repairs
  2. Health benefits
    • People are more likely to walking or cycle to work if they live close
    • Both walking and cycling are healthier than commuting by car
  3. Environmental benefits
    • Cars cause more pollution than walking/ cycling
    • Concluding sentence: People should look for a job that’s close to home

Full Paragraph

Working close to home offers many advantages compared to living far from work. First, it is better financially. A shorter commute will reduce the amount of money a person is likely to spend on gas and on car repairs. Secondly, living close to work is healthier for people. It provides them with more opportunities to walk or ride a bicycle to work, which are both great forms of exercise. Moreover, these forms of transportation are better for the environment than a lengthy car commute. In conclusion, the ideal job is the one that’s located close to home.

The connections between the outline and the paragraph are clear, and the writer uses transitional words and phrases for coherence. However, the writer can make this a better paragraph by supporting the various claims with evidence from other sources. It has been indicated by bold type where the reader may need more detail in order to be persuaded by the writer’s argument.

Advanced Depth

The first letter of the first word from the source can be changed to an upper or lower case letter to fit your sentence.

Paragraph Outline

Topic sentence: Living close to work has many advantages.

  1. Financial benefits
    • Spend less gas
      • CAA estimates: $1700 per year on gas, compact car, 20,000 kms (Source: CAA.ca, “Car costs”)
    • Spend less on car repairs
      • Spend less on regular maintenance (engine oil, air filter, transmission fluid)
      • Plus avoid larger repairs due to wear-and-tear or collisions
  2. Health benefits
    • People are more likely to walking or cycle to work if they live close
    • Both walking and cycling are healthier than commuting by car
      • Regular daily exercise can improve health (source: J. Gill & C. Celis-Morales, “Cycling to work: major new study suggests health benefits are staggering”, The Conversation, April 19 2017, paras. 8-11)
        • Cycling to work vs. car/ public transport
          1. 52% lower risk of dying from heart disease
          2. 40% lower risk of dying from cancer
          3. Lower risks of developing heart disease / cancer at all
        • Similar benefits for walkers
  3. Environmental benefits
    • Alternative forms of transportation create less pollution than travelling by car
      • Each trip: Transit riders → 65% less GHG emissions (Source: Canadian Urban Transit Association, Issue Paper #16: “Public Transit – A Climate Change Solution”, p. 2)
Concluding sentence: Living close to home can actually make a big difference, for individuals and for the earth

Full Paragraph

Working close to home offers many advantages compared to living far from work. First, it is better financially. A shorter commute will reduce the amount of money a person is likely to spend on gas, which can cost the average Canadian driver almost $2000 per year (CAA, 2016, “Car costs”). A shorter commute would also help drivers reduce the cost of regular maintenance and avoid larger repairs due to wear-and-tear or collisions. Secondly, living close to work can encourage people to live a healthier lifestyle because it provides them with more opportunities to walk or ride a bicycle to work.According to a recent study, swapping the car for a bike can dramatically reduce a person’s chance of dying from major illnesses like heart disease and cancer (Gill & Cellis-Morales, 2017, paras. 8-11). In addition to these personal benefits, people can also help to reduce air pollution when they live close enough to work that they can leave their car behind: commuting to work by public transit, for example, produces 65% less greenhouse gas emissions than the same trip taken by car. Clearly, moving closer to work is about more than just convenience. Instead, it’s a move that could have positive implications for the entire planet, as well for our bodies and our wallets

The primary advantage with adding depth to the outline and the full paragraph is to provide a more compelling argument for the reader to agree with your position. The added evidence is indicated by bold type. Since the writer uses evidence to back up their claims, they can draw a bolder conclusion than the basic example.
