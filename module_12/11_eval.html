Completing the Research Process — Part 1

Assignment: Research Project Part 3: Final Draft

Due Date: Last day of week 13, 11:55 p.m. See Calendar or Instructional Plan.
Value of Assignment: 20% of final grade
Passing Grade: 55%
Length: Approximately 1100-1200 words
Submission Instructions: Submit your work as a .doc/.docx or .pdf file to the assignment submission folder titled “Research Project Part 3 – Final Draft”. Please do not submit your work as a zipped folder.
Evaluation: See below for details.

This assignment is an opportunity for you to write the report that expresses your answer to the research question you set for yourself.
This is an individual assignment.

Rationale

Your purpose in this task is to complete your research project, which will include:

  • Employing critical thinking to analyse source, voice, bias, meaning, argument, and evidence
  • Applying the three-step process (plan, write, revise) to create a variety of academic and workplace documents
  • Applying knowledge of standard Canadian English Grammar, spelling, and punctuation to academic and professional writing

Evaluation

The rubric for assessing this assignment is here (quicklink to pdf or Word document).

The policy for late submission of student work can be found in the Course Information module in this course.

Directions

    1Based on the topic you had approved for your proposal assignment and using the comprehensive outline you have already created, write a report that expresses your answer to the research question you set for yourself.

    2Consider the purpose and audience you identified in your proposal. What information needs to be included? What strategies will suit your topic best?

    3Once you have finished planning your paper, write a draft, revise it for organization and content, proofread it for mechanics and grammar, and then submit it to the appropriate eConestoga drop box for an originality check to ensure the documentation and paraphrasing are correct.

    4Double-check your paper using the checklist providedinsert quicklink to topic page: Checklist for Research Paper

    5Use APA@Conestoga format for your paper:

    • Use 12-point Arial font.
    • Double space your analysis.
    • Include a title page according to this format from The Learning Commons.
    • Include a Reference Page following APA@Conestoga documentation style.
    • Remember! Cite your sources using both in-text citations and a References page.

    6Revise your paper to ensure it reads clearly and that it follows the rubric and the assignment instructions.
