What is the Trouble with the Truth?

The term online learning refers to a number of different approaches using numerous different online environments. For the purpose of this module we will focus on an online environment within a learning management system (LMS).

Analysis 1

This paraphrase is ineffective.

This writer has used a thesaurus to replace key words with other similar words in order to avoid repeating three of the original words in a row.

  • "Extent" became "amplitude"
  • "Automation" became "computerization"
  • "Affect" became "impinge"
  • "Employment" became "enrollment"
  • "Controversial" became "litigious"

The result is ineffective because many of the new words carry a different meaning from the original. Combined, the words produce a sentence thatâ€™s even more dense and difficult to understand than the original sentence.

Example 2

Experts are still debating whether employment will be hurt by automation in the long run (Sorenson, 2013, para.8)

Is this paraphrase effective? Click the arrow to check your answer.

Analysis 2

This paraphrase is effective.

Although this paraphrase includes a few individual words from the original, such as "employment" and "automation", it avoids using phrases like "the extent to which".

It also presents the ideas in a different order than the original. The original sentence ended with "controversial", while the paraphrase begins with "experts are still debating".

The meaning and the intent of the message are retained while the words and order have changed.
