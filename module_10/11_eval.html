Proposing Research

Assignment: Research Project Part 1: Proposal Email

Due Date: Last day of week 10, 11:55 p.m. See Calendar or Instructional Plan.
Value of Assignment: 5% of final grade
Passing Grade: 55%
Length: 5 paragraphs
Submission Instructions: Submit your work as a .doc/.docx or .pdf file to the assignment submission folder titled “Research Project Part 1 – Proposal Email”. Please do not submit your work as a zipped folder.
Evaluation: See below.

This assignment is an opportunity for you to present your research question as a proposal email to the instructor. Your purpose in this task is to get your instructor’s approval and feedback before you begin your research project.

This is an individual assignment.

Rationale

This assignment is an opportunity to describe your plan for the research project by:

  • Identifying purpose, audience, and thesis in a variety of texts
  • Employing critical thinking to analyse source, voice, bias, meaning, argument, and evidence
  • Applying the three-step process (plan, write, revise) to create a variety of academic and workplace documents

Directions

    1Identify a topic or issue that is relevant to your field of study and of particular interest to you. This will be the topic for your research project due later in the course.

    • In this task, you are free to propose any topic or issue that is relevant to your field of study. It’s also recommended that this topic be something that you are familiar with and interested in.

    2Narrow this topic or issue by focusing on a particular research question that you would like to answer in your final research assignment.

    3Use the LRC databases to find materials that will help you to answer your question.

    4Use the email format that was laid out in week 10’s content to present your research question. This template will help you to ensure that you are providing your instructor with a clear and detailed outline of your plan.

    • Use the template in topic page: The Proposal Email, but do not copy whole phrases.
    • Use 12-point Arial font.

    5Revise your email to ensure it reads clearly and that it follows the rubric, the assignment instructions, and the resources provided in eConestoga in week 10.
