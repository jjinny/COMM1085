Proposing Research

Activity: Finding the Appropriate Focus

For the research assignment, you are being asked to develop your own research question rather than select and respond to a predetermined question. Developing a strong and interesting research question is a crucial first step in this process because it will give you something specific to search for: an complete and accurate answer to your question. When you have found this answer, it will become the main focus of your research project.

There is only one requirement placed upon your planning: you must locate your research question broadly within your field of study, program area, or future line of work.

You may find it easier to find an appropriate focus for your project if you imagine you are looking at your program area through the lens of your own personal interests:

  • What issues in your field of study are most compelling to you?
  • Which questions seem most urgent?
  • Which ideas are going to be most important in the future?

The answers to these questions will help lead you toward a rewarding research question.
