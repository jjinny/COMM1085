Proposing Research

Week 10 Summary

The process of writing a research paper includes many steps from choosing a topic to revising the final draft. This week, we looked at the first two steps: choosing a topic and planning the research.

1 Choosing a topic:

  • Consider ways in which your personal interests and your field of study overlap
  • Once a broad topic suggests itself, brainstorm, mapping topics and sub-topics to show potential connections
  • Frame your research goals as a question. Make sure your question avoids the following pitfalls:
    • too broad; too narrow; closed; subjective; leading; multiple

2 Planning the research:
An excellent way to make sure you have planned every aspect of your research is to write a proposal that describes what you will do. This also allows your professor to give you helpful feedback to improve your research results. A good proposal provides the following:

  • An informative subject line
  • A simple explanation of the research topic
  • A discussion of why the research is relevant
  • The research question phrased as a question
  • A description of potential research sources
  • The type of analytic strategy that best answers the research question
    • process/ classification/ cause-effect/ comparison/ persuasion/ argumentation
  • A timeline of how the research, writing, and revision will be accomplished
  • A request for permission to proceed with your research
